USE CASE
NENIA X TEMCO

TEMCO’s business operation

TEMCO is developing a blockchain based supply chain management platform. By using blockchain technology, segregated supply chain network is connected under one platform. Utilizing the data stored in the blockchain, TEMCO offers an analytics tool for businesses to make better strategic decisions on their supply chain management processes. For consumers, TEMCO provides real-time supply chain management information and an ecommerce platform with cryptocurrency transaction capabilities.


Why TEMCO applied blockchain technology on supply chain network?

Existing supply chain management system is siloed, and information is disconnected among supply chain participants. To solve this problem, TEMCO is taking advantage of the blockchain technology to connect every participant under one platform from manufacturing to final consumers.

About the case study

Partnering with Nenia, a producer/retailer of eco-friendly organic food, TEMCO linked all the information from the initial product production stage to final delivery step using blockchain. The TEMCO platform enables Nenia to depart from their siloed systems and to benefit from efficient data management and higher customer satisfaction.

Nenia uses an ERP system for product management. Due to the nature of ERP being individualized and isolated, the connection of entire supply chain process is not smooth enough to determine where the problems occur at each stage. In a case of any problems, using the existing system is difficult to find and validate where and how the problem originated. Also, consumers were not able to realize and appreciate the company’s eco-friendly organic product value proposition. By using TEMCO platform, Nenia acquired transparency of their supply chain network and such information was also made available to consumers.

Nenia’s current system & opportunities

Nenia was ideal for the use case study because the company is directly responsible for the entire process from purchasing of organic ingredients to arrival of the products at a retail store. However, under the current system, it was not easy to gain connected supply chain information and deliver eco-friendly image to consumers. To strengthen the competitiveness of Nenia’s specialty, TEMCO suggested blockchain technology that allows all the participants of the supply chain to access the journey of Nenia's products.

Pilot Phase 1
Manufacturing Facility:
QR code Generation and
Product Registration

All Nenia’s products are sourced from organic ingredients. One of the most popular items is “Woori-mil dumplings”, which is made from 100% pesticide-free Korean wheat and antibiotic-free pork.


Locally grown organic wheat and antibiotic-free pig


Although Nenia produces high quality and healthy food products from organic ingredients such as certified antibiotic-free pork and locally produced Korean wheat, do consumers trust Nenia’s products and purchase from them?

According to the Label Insight Food Revolution study in 2016, 94% of consumers picked product ingredient and transparency in product production as important criteria for purchasing food products.


Organic certification of antibiotic-free pork and pesticide-free wheat


Imagine how the world will change if manufacturers, supply chain participants, and final consumers can all share the same information. TEMCO and Nenia are progressively solving problems stated earlier using supply chain management through blockchain. As a start, raw ingredient information for “Woori-mil dumplings” was uploaded to the blockchain.


Food washing process

Organic vegetables are thoroughly washed with clean water and peeled off prior to entering dumpling manufacturing process


Verified by data stored in the blockchain, organic raw ingredients are transformed into highly trusted food products through an eco-friendly manufacturing process. All products go through HACCP and are manufactured in a sanitary and quality-controlled environment. The products are packaged in the same manner. Because of such effort, consumers are no longer worried about the food quality.


Dumplings are processed in a sanitary and clean environment


In the existing system, product information such as antibiotic-free indication is lightly displayed on the product packages. However, this information is limited in offering details to the consumers, so Through it is not effective on realizing the quality of the products. Using blockchain enables storing of complete product information including certifications (eg. Non-antibiotic, pesticide-free, GAP, etc.) and other important facts. By sharing of the information with everyone, Nenia’s products become more accessible and trusted by final consumers.


Dumpling manufacturing process ends with clean sealed packaging


To make this process possible, QR code used for uploading data to the blockchain will be easily printed from the TEMCO application and attached to the product package so that information about the product can be recorded and verified. By scanning the QR code, anyone can easily access the product information.


Nenia’s employee registering product information through TEMCO’s vender application


All the major product information is initially uploaded to the blockchain before arriving to the main distribution facility, and it becomes the basis for tracking the product at each stage.

Pilot Phase 2
The Distribution Facility:
Product Transfer

All manufactured products are shipped to Nenia’s distribution facility in Gwangju, Korea. Each product is stored based on the product’s characteristics such as temperature and humidity. Since all Nenia’s products do not contain chemical additives, they are more sensitive to the storage environment. Typically, Nenia’s products are refrigerated or stored in the freezer.


Nenia’s office staff running verification process for received shipments from the manufacturing factory


As these products arrive to the distribution facility, they are stored and maintained under a strict guideline using ERP. The company also conducts inventory check twice a month to verify the validity of their own assets. However, when conducting inventory check in the current system, it takes a lot of time and effort, also increases management costs.


Nenia’s employees conducting inventory check and regular inspection


Using blockchain to store all the information including inventory records will enable Nenia to efficiently manage its inventory and allow it to improve its quality control process without incurring extra effort and cost.


Nenia's All-Natural and Organic Dumpling


Products in the distribution facility are shipped out to the retailers such as the company-owned store in Bukchon, Seoul and other franchises when orders come in. For frozen products, dry ice is added to the package to maintain the frozen state until they arrive to the retailers and/or customers. After the packaging process, the blockchain based QR codes are placed on the packaging or boxes, which will have all the data connecting from manufacturing of the product to the storage, and the storage to ship out stage, all in one.


Products are being prepared for second shipping to retail stores


After the products are packaged, the shipments undergo a final quality evaluation before getting shipped out. Such process further increases consumers’ trust in Nenia’s products and its brand.


All Nenia's products are transported within a cold chain and stored at low temperature

Potential benefits using TEMCO platform

Manufactures can now understand where and how their products are transferred all at once. Blockchain based supply chain network informs where the problems occurred, and participants can quickly find out how to solve the problems. In addition, manufacturers can check the storage condition through TEMCO’s application regardless of the place. Through blockchain based supply chain network, the manufacturer can maintain the high quality of the products and communicate with the responsible party to address any issues or problems. Through such effort, businesses will gain consumer’s trust by offering higher quality products.

TEMCO’s vendor application will simplify the process of uploading data. The application is also easy to use as well, it can be used by anyone. By using the application, no manual entry is needed, reducing any human errors. With the innovation of blockchain and IoT technology, information can be automatically entered and thus, eliminate redundancy of manual input and validation.

Until now, accessing product information and supply chain data was difficult. However, scanning the QR code through TEMCO’s consumer application will not only show basic product information, but also raw ingredients used, manufacture information, distribution records and shipping data. This complete connection of all data will reduce the barrier in purchasing and will eventually lead to higher customer satisfaction.

More information about products are available through TEMCO’s consumer application, and this will increase the probability of consumer purchases. Businesses like Nenia can benefit greatly from the TEMCO platform, gaining consumer loyalty and brand awareness.
